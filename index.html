Healthcare Facilities & Insurance Coverage Dashboard West Java Province

Project Overview

This dashboard visualizes data on healthcare facility distribution and health insurance coverage across West Java Province. The aim is to identify gaps in healthcare access and assess the equity of public health services across different cities and regencies, providing data-driven insights for policymakers and stakeholders.

Raw Dataset Link

Google Sheets Dataset

Tableau Dashboard

Insights & Findings

  • Total healthcare facilities: 55,670 units across 27 cities/regencies
  • A striking 94.74% of these are Posyandu (community-based health posts), highlighting strong grassroots health efforts
  • Bogor Regency and Bandung Regency have the highest number of facilities
  • Bogor Regency also has the largest insured population (3.5 million people)
  • In terms of insurance type:
    • Government-subsidized insurance (PBI-APBN) dominates at 43.37%
    • Employees under wage scheme (PPU): 23.08%
    • Self-employed/non-wage workers (PBPU): 18.13%
    • Remaining population is covered by local schemes (Jamkesda), private insurance, or employer-based plans
